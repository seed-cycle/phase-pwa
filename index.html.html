Rituals
Simple practices to align with the moon and your cycle.
New Moon Ritual
Set your intentions
The New Moon is a time for new beginnings. Write down three intentions for this cycle — what you want to call in, create, or release. This is also when you switch to Phase 1 seeds (Flaxseed + Pumpkin).

Light a candle. Sit quietly. Write without overthinking.
Follicular Phase Ritual
Fresh flowers & new energy
As oestrogen rises, so does your vitality. Buy yourself fresh flowers. Start the project you've been putting off. Go somewhere new. Your body is literally designed to explore and create right now — follow it.
Full Moon Ritual
Moon gazing & appreciation
Step outside tonight and look up. The Full Moon marks the midpoint — a moment of fullness, clarity, and release. Write down what you appreciate from the past two weeks — moments, people, shifts in your body. Switch to your Phase 2 seeds (Sesame + Sunflower) tomorrow.
Luteal Phase Ritual
Slow down on purpose
Your body is asking for warmth and quietness. Make a nourishing soup or stew. Cancel something optional. Take a long bath. This isn't doing nothing — it's doing exactly what your hormones need right now.

About Phase
Phase by The Seed Cycle
Version 1.0
Phase is a daily companion for seed cycling and cycle awareness — built on the understanding that your body, the moon and the earth's seasons all move in rhythm together.
Seed cycling is a gentle nutritional practice: flaxseeds and pumpkin seeds in the first half of your cycle, sesame and sunflower seeds in the second. A daily act of nourishment — not a medical treatment. Give it three full cycles.
The rituals, meditations and nervous system practices in this app are invitations, not prescriptions.
Phase is informed by the work of many — to name a few: Jane Hardwicke Collings (Thirteen Moons), Jema Lee (Wellsome & Cyclical School), Alisa Vitti (In the Flo), Dr Jolene Brighten (Beyond the Pill), Miranda Gray (Red Moon) — and by The Seed Cycle's own conversations with thousands of women navigating their cycles. It is not a medical app and does not replace professional healthcare advice.
